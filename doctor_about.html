About Blood Cancer Diagnosis

For Doctors

Welcome, {{ doctor_username }}! This platform is designed to assist medical professionals in the early detection and management of blood cancer, specifically leukemia.

Our Mission

We aim to provide doctors with advanced tools for leukemia detection using deep learning models like VGG16 and U-Net, enabling accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment plans.

Features for Doctors

  • Upload blood smear images for automated leukemia detection.
  • View detailed results with cancer cell markings and confidence scores.
  • Generate and download patient reports directly from the platform.
  • Access medication and treatment guidelines tailored for leukemia stages.

Technology

Our system leverages state-of-the-art deep learning models, including VGG16 for classification and U-Net for segmentation, to identify leukemia stages (Benign, Malignant Early, Malignant Pre, Malignant Pro) with high accuracy.

Leukemia Stages

Benign

The Benign stage indicates no presence of leukemia. However, it may include non-cancerous blood disorders such as anemia or infections that mimic early leukemia symptoms.

Causes of benign conditions mimicking leukemia:

  • Infections (e.g., viral or bacterial) leading to temporary blood cell abnormalities.
  • Nutritional deficiencies like iron deficiency anemia.
  • Autoimmune disorders affecting blood cell production.

Malignant Early

The Malignant Early stage is the initial phase of leukemia, characterized by the presence of a small number of abnormal white blood cells that begin to multiply uncontrollably.

Causes of Malignant Early leukemia:

  • Genetic mutations (e.g., chromosomal abnormalities like the Philadelphia chromosome).
  • Exposure to radiation or chemotherapy from previous cancer treatments.
  • Environmental factors like benzene exposure.

Malignant Pre

The Malignant Pre stage shows intermediate progression of leukemia, with a significant increase in abnormal cells, often affecting bone marrow function and causing symptoms like fatigue and infections.

Causes of Malignant Pre leukemia progression:

  • Untreated early-stage leukemia allowing abnormal cells to proliferate.
  • Genetic predispositions, such as family history of leukemia.
  • Immune system suppression, increasing susceptibility to cancer progression.

Malignant Pro

The Malignant Pro stage is the advanced phase of leukemia, where aggressive cancerous cells dominate the bone marrow and blood, leading to severe symptoms and complications.

Causes of Malignant Pro leukemia:

  • Advanced genetic mutations leading to aggressive cancer cell growth.
  • Prolonged exposure to carcinogens (e.g., tobacco, radiation).
  • Failure of early treatment, allowing leukemia to advance to a severe stage.
